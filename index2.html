LAST DETECTIVE STANDING

SITCOM DYSTOPIAN MYSTERY COMEDY

"A detective who refuses to retire, even after the world ends."

When the world ended, his job began.

GENERAL CONCEPT

LAST DETECTIVE STANDING is a sitcom set 20 years after the collapse of civilization. In the ruins of a once-thriving city, a small group of survivors has created a relatively stable community: New Hope.

At the center of this community is Jack Murphy, a once-brilliant detective, now cynical and jaded. Jack was at the peak of his career when the world collapsed. Today, at 58, he stubbornly maintains his role as a detective in a world where traditional crimes seem absurd compared to the daily challenges of survival.

Ironically, the residents of New Hope continue to bring him "cases" to solve – but these investigations are often ridiculously mundane or completely out of touch with post-apocalyptic reality. Jack finds himself searching for lost items, resolving neighborly disputes, or investigating food thefts, all while handling his worn notebook and faded detective hat.

The humor of the series comes from the disconnect between Jack's aspirations to be a "real detective" in the film noir tradition, and the absurdity of the situations he faces in this devastated world. Despite everything, these seemingly trivial investigations often reveal profound truths about human nature and the secrets hidden in the ruins of civilization.

"In the old world, I solved murders. Now I track down missing chickens. The apocalypse really ruined my career."

— Jack Murphy

MAIN PLOT

The Investigation Begins

The story begins when Ellie, a 25-year-old woman, brings Jack a necklace she found in the ruins. This necklace with a strange pendant seems ordinary, but Ellie insists that it "sometimes glows at night" and that she's "heard voices" since finding it.

Jack, accustomed to eccentric requests, reluctantly accepts the case, more to have something to do than out of genuine interest. Yet, as he investigates the origins of the necklace, he discovers troubling clues that link it to his own past and to the catastrophic event that destroyed civilization.

The Unlikely Team

Throughout the episodes, Jack and his unlikely team of assistants (all residents of New Hope) investigate the mystery of the necklace while managing the comic daily problems of their community. Each episode combines a "case of the week" often absurd with the progressive advancement of the main plot around the necklace.

The Secret Revealed

The necklace turns out to be an advanced technological key that could access a secret bunker containing vital resources, but also potentially answers about the catastrophe that wiped out the world – an event mysteriously linked to an unsolved investigation of Jack's just before the apocalypse.

SERIES TIMELINE

The major events that shape the world of "Last Detective Standing":

20 Years Ago: The Catastrophe

A mysterious global event causes widespread destruction and collapse of civilization. Only small pockets of humanity survive in isolated communities.

18 Years Ago: New Hope Founded

Jack and a small group of survivors establish the community of New Hope in the ruins of a former suburban area. Basic governance and survival systems are put in place.

15 Years Ago: First Contact with Salvagers

New Hope encounters the Salvagers for the first time – a technologically advanced group of survivors who scavenge pre-catastrophe technology. Initial relations are tense but non-violent.

10 Years Ago: The First Murder

Jack investigates his first (and only) murder case in New Hope when a community member is found dead. The case remains technically unsolved, though Jack has his suspicions.

5 Years Ago: Herbert Becomes Mayor

Herbert Jenkins appoints himself "Mayor" of New Hope and begins implementing bureaucratic systems reminiscent of pre-catastrophe government, much to Jack's annoyance.

Present Day: Ellie's Arrival

Ellie arrives in New Hope with the mysterious necklace, setting in motion the events of the series and reopening wounds from the past.

CHARACTERS

JACK MURPHY

Role: Protagonist, washed-up detective

Lead Anti-Hero

A former renowned police inspector, Jack is 58 years old and stubbornly clings to his detective identity despite the collapse of the world. Sarcastic, gruff but deeply human, he hides his sensitivity behind unwavering cynicism. He still wears his worn fedora, patched trench coat, and never parts with his nearly full notebook.

"In the old world, I solved murders. Now I track down missing chickens. The apocalypse really ruined my career."

Character Background

Jack was once the star detective of the city's police department, known for solving high-profile cases. He was married to Dr. Emily Murphy, a brilliant physicist working on experimental technology for a secretive government project. Their daughter was born just before the catastrophe.

Jack's last case before the world ended involved investigating the disappearance of several scientists, including his wife. He was close to uncovering a conspiracy when the catastrophe struck, leaving him with guilt and unanswered questions.

Character Development

Initially, Jack is cynical and emotionally closed-off, going through the motions of being a detective mainly to preserve his identity. As the season progresses, his investigation into the necklace reignites his passion and skill for detective work.

His relationship with Ellie evolves from reluctant mentor to something deeper as he unconsciously recognizes something familiar in her. The central emotional arc is Jack learning to confront his past and open himself to connection again.

Casting Suggestion

Timothy Olyphant would bring the perfect blend of grit, dry humor, and hidden depth to Jack's character.

ELLIE CHEN

Role: Mysterious client, plot catalyst

Co-Lead Mystery

A young woman of 25 born just after the catastrophe, Ellie is curious, fearless, and fascinated by the "world before" she never knew. After discovering the mysterious necklace, she becomes Jack's unofficial assistant, determined to solve this mystery. Her optimism perfectly contrasts with Jack's cynicism.

"You know what's great about the apocalypse? No bills to pay, no emails to answer. Just the daily struggle not to die... Relaxing, isn't it?"

Character Background

Ellie was born just days before the catastrophe. She believes she was orphaned during the chaos and raised by various survivor groups until she joined the Salvagers as a teenager. Intelligent and adaptable, she excelled at reconnaissance missions.

Her entire life has been spent in the post-apocalyptic world, giving her a unique perspective that combines pragmatic survival skills with an almost childlike wonder about the "old world" she never experienced.

Character Development

Ellie begins the series with a hidden agenda involving the necklace, sent by the Salvagers to infiltrate New Hope. However, as she works with Jack and forms connections with the community, she starts questioning her mission and loyalties.

Throughout the season, she experiences strange memories and visions that hint at her true identity. Her journey is one of self-discovery and choosing her own path versus the one laid out for her by others.

Casting Suggestion

Ella Purnell would perfectly capture Ellie's blend of toughness, vulnerability, and mysterious quality.

DR. MARCUS RODRIGUEZ

Role: Community physician/scientist

Supporting Comic Relief

A former biology professor turned doctor by necessity, Marcus is Jack's "expert" for all technical or medical questions. A hypochondriac despite his scientific training, he is constantly worried about germs and diseases, which is paradoxical in an already devastated world. His improvised laboratory becomes the informal HQ of the team.

"You want me to analyze this potentially radioactive object with... a children's chemistry kit and a magnifying glass? Okay, give me five minutes."

Character Background

Marcus was a brilliant but socially awkward biology professor at the local university before the catastrophe. Having no medical training but extensive scientific knowledge, he became New Hope's de facto doctor when they couldn't find a real physician.

His hypochondria developed after the catastrophe as a psychological response to the sudden lack of modern medicine and sanitation. Despite this, he's extraordinarily brave when others need him, often overcoming his fears to help the community.

Character Development

Marcus begins as primarily comic relief but gradually reveals deeper layers. He harbors a secret fascination with pre-catastrophe technology that borders on obsession, making his analysis of the necklace particularly significant.

Throughout the season, he develops more confidence in his abilities while also forming an unexpected friendship with Ruby, who helps him overcome some of his fears through unorthodox methods.

Casting Suggestion

Kumail Nanjiani would bring the perfect combination of neurotic energy, intellectual sharpness, and hidden courage to Marcus.

RUBY "TANK" WILLIAMS

Role: Head of security for New Hope

Supporting Action

A former MMA champion turned bodyguard, Ruby is the physical force of the group. She ensures the security of New Hope but is deeply bored and willingly joins Jack's investigations to find action. Beneath her intimidating appearance lies an unexpected passion for poetry and philosophy.

"I can neutralize a man in twelve different ways with this spoon. But I'd rather use it for my tea, if you don't mind."

Character Background

Ruby was on the verge of becoming a world champion MMA fighter when the catastrophe occurred. She initially survived by protecting a group of children she found abandoned, using her fighting skills to keep them safe in the chaotic aftermath.

Her nickname "Tank" was given to her by the kids she protected, who now grown, still live in New Hope. Her tough exterior hides both her intellectual interests and the trauma of losing her wife during the catastrophe.

Character Development

Ruby initially sees her security role as merely functional, taking little joy in it. Through the season, as she helps with investigations, she rediscovers purpose and the satisfaction of using her skills for more than just routine patrols.

Her hidden intellectual side becomes more prominent as the series progresses, with her philosophical insights often providing unexpected perspectives on their investigations. She forms an unlikely friendship with Marcus that brings out different sides of both characters.

Casting Suggestion

Danai Gurira would excellently portray Ruby's physical prowess, hidden depths, and nuanced characterization.

HERBERT "HERB" JENKINS

Role: Self-proclaimed mayor of New Hope

Supporting Foil

A former insurance salesman turned administrative leader of the community, Herb is obsessed with rules, procedures, and paperwork – even if none of it makes sense anymore. He desperately tries to maintain an illusion of bureaucratic normalcy and considers Jack a "civil servant" who should report to him.

"No, you can't just 'go investigate.' There's a form to fill out. In triplicate. Yes, even after the apocalypse."

Character Background

Herbert was a mid-level insurance salesman before the catastrophe, unremarkable and largely overlooked. The apocalypse, strangely, became his opportunity to reinvent himself as someone important.

His insistence on bureaucracy and procedure stems from genuine belief that structure is what separates humans from animals, and that maintaining "civilization" means maintaining its systems—even when those systems no longer serve a practical purpose.

Character Development

Initially presented as a comedic obstacle for Jack, Herbert's character deepens as we learn his bureaucratic obsession comes from witnessing chaos in the early days after the catastrophe. His seemingly ridiculous rules actually helped establish order in New Hope.

Throughout the season, Herbert struggles with the changing reality as the investigation reveals truths about the past. His journey involves learning to adapt his love of order to a world that requires flexibility.

Casting Suggestion

Tony Hale would brilliantly capture Herbert's bureaucratic fussiness while also revealing the deeper motivations behind his character.

THE MYSTERIOUS NECKLACE

Role: Central plot object

Plot Device Mystery

Much more than a simple piece of jewelry, this antique-looking necklace contains hidden advanced technology. Its triangular pendant sometimes illuminates and seems to react to certain places or people. It becomes almost a character in its own right, whose mystery is gradually revealed throughout the episodes.

"Great. A glowing trinket. Exactly what we need in a world without electricity or running water."

— Jack, holding the necklace

Object Specifications

The necklace appears to be an antique silver chain with a triangular pendant made of an unknown metallic material. The pendant contains intricate engravings that seem technological rather than decorative. At its center is a small gem-like object that occasionally emits a blue-green glow.

As the series progresses, the necklace displays increasingly advanced properties: projecting images, responding to specific locations, and storing what appears to be recorded messages or memories.

Narrative Significance

The necklace serves multiple narrative functions: it's a mystery for Jack to solve, a connection to the pre-apocalypse world, and ultimately the key to understanding the catastrophe itself.

Its true nature as experimental temporal technology created by Jack's wife links all the main plot threads together: Jack's past, Ellie's identity, the catastrophe, and the future of New Hope.

Visual Design Concept

The necklace design blends antique aesthetics with subtle futuristic elements, appearing both familiar and alien simultaneously. Its glow effect would be a distinctive visual signature throughout the series.

CENTRAL CONFLICT

The main conflict of the series operates on several levels:

External Immediate Conflict

Jack and his improvised team must discover the truth about the mysterious necklace while facing a rival group, "The Salvagers," a faction of technologically advanced survivors who are also trying to get their hands on the necklace.

Community Conflict

The small society of New Hope is constantly threatened by limited resources, internal tensions, and external dangers. Jack must balance his personal quest with his responsibilities to the community.

Jack's Internal Conflict

As the investigation progresses, Jack is confronted with his own demons and the possibility that he played an unwitting role in the catastrophe that destroyed the world. His struggle to maintain his detective identity in a world that no longer needs detectives becomes a metaphor for human attachment to the past.

What begins as a simple investigation into a curious object progressively becomes an existential quest about responsibility, redemption, and the possibility of rebuilding a better world from the ashes of the old.

THE TWIST

SPOILER ALERT

At the end of the first season, a major twist upends everything we thought we knew:

The Revelation of the Necklace

The necklace is not simply a key to a bunker - it's an experimental temporal control technology. The "voices" Ellie heard were temporal echoes from the past and future. The bunker it opens contains a facility that could potentially alter the course of time.

Ellie's True Identity

Ellie is not simply a young woman who found the necklace by chance. She is actually Jack's daughter, born just before the catastrophe and whom he believed dead. She was raised by The Salvagers and sent to find Jack, without knowing their true kinship. She pursues her own mission: discovering the truth about her origins.

Jack's Last Case

The last case Jack was working on before the catastrophe was related to the disappearance of a scientist working on a secret government project. This scientist was Ellie's mother and Jack's wife. This investigation had led him to discover a plot involving experimental technology that eventually caused the global catastrophe - the same type of technology contained in the necklace.

The Final Scene Reveal

In the final moments of Season 1, after all revelations, the necklace activates fully, creating a temporal window. Through this window, we glimpse an intact pre-catastrophe world, and a woman who looks remarkably like an older version of Ellie. She stares directly at them and says: "Jack, I've been waiting for you to find it. Now we can fix what we broke." This suggests that time travel may be possible, opening endless narrative possibilities for Season 2.

VISUAL STYLE & WORLD-BUILDING

Visual Aesthetic

Cinematography

The series blends two distinct visual styles:

  1. Warm, handheld camera work with natural lighting for community scenes, creating an intimate, documentary-like feel.
  2. Film noir-inspired compositions with dramatic shadows and framing for Jack's "detective" moments, often contrasting humorously with the mundane nature of his cases.

Flashbacks to the pre-catastrophe world are shot with oversaturated colors and smooth camera movements, emphasizing the stark contrast with the present day.

Mood Board

Key Locations

New Hope

A former suburban neighborhood transformed into a walled community. Homes have been reinforced and modified, with solar panels and water collection systems. Community gardens occupy former lawns, and the central park serves as both marketplace and meeting grounds.

Jack's "Office"

A converted garage with faded police posters and a makeshift desk. Case files are meticulously organized despite their irrelevance. A worn leather chair and vintage desk lamp complete his attempt to recreate a classic detective's office.

The Ruins

The collapsed downtown area of the former city, now overgrown with vegetation. Skyscrapers lean precariously, and streets are fractured. This dangerous zone harbors valuable resources but also unknown threats.

Key Art Concept

Key Visual Elements

  • Jack stands amid ruins in his trademark trench coat and fedora
  • The mysterious necklace glows prominently in the foreground
  • The New Hope community visible in the background
  • Split lighting: warm community lights vs. cold blue ruins

TARGET AUDIENCE & BUSINESS CASE

Primary Audience Profiles

Sci-Fi/Post-Apocalyptic Fans

25-44 year olds who enjoy shows like "The Last of Us," "Station Eleven," and "Fallout." They appreciate complex world-building and dystopian settings with a fresh twist.

Comedy Drama Enthusiasts

18-49 year olds who enjoy genre-blending shows with both humor and emotional depth, like "The Good Place," "Barry," and "Russian Doll."

Mystery/Detective Show Fans

30-55 year olds who enjoy mystery-solving narratives like "True Detective," "Only Murders in the Building," and "Poker Face," but are looking for fresh settings.

Business Value Proposition

Production Feasibility

Despite its post-apocalyptic setting, the series is designed to be cost-effective:

  • Limited locations (New Hope community, ruins, bunker)
  • Small core cast with featured extras
  • Practical effects prioritized over CGI
  • Sitcom-styled production approach for community scenes

Expansion Potential

The concept offers rich opportunities for franchise expansion:

  • Multiple seasons with evolving mystery (5-season arc planned)
  • Potential spin-offs focused on other communities
  • Transmedia opportunities (podcast about "cases," interactive mystery)
  • Merchandise possibilities (detective kit, necklace replicas)

Competitive Advantage

"Last Detective Standing" stands out in the crowded streaming landscape by:

  • Combining popular genres (post-apocalyptic, mystery, comedy) in a fresh way
  • Offering both episodic satisfaction and long-arc mystery
  • Providing a unique tone that balances humor with genuine emotion and suspense
  • Creating immediately distinctive and memeable characters
  • Appealing to multiple demographic groups simultaneously

WHY THIS SERIES WILL BE A SUCCESS

Unique Genre Blend

The unprecedented combination of a sitcom with a dystopian setting and noir detective elements creates a unique visual and narrative experience. This contrast allows exploring dark themes while maintaining an accessible and entertaining tone.

Endearing Characters

The colorful and deeply human characters offer multiple identification points for the audience. Their interactions naturally create comic situations, while exploring their traumas and hopes in this devastated world.

Addictive Narrative Structure

Each episode offers a "case of the week" resolved in a sitcom format, while progressively developing the central mystery of the necklace and Jack's past. This dual narrative structure encourages binge-watching to discover the next piece of the puzzle.

Comic and Dramatic Potential

The absurdity of the situations—a private detective in a world without formal laws—offers fertile ground for humor. At the same time, the emotional stakes and themes of loss, identity, and redemption bring a dramatic depth that resonates with the audience.

Expansion Potential

The post-apocalyptic universe offers countless possibilities for exploration. Future seasons could introduce new communities, explore other aspects of the devastated world, or even incorporate elements of time travel following the twist of the first season.

Broad Demographic Target

The series can attract both fans of classic sitcoms, dystopian science fiction enthusiasts, and detective mystery lovers. Its accessible yet intelligent tone can appeal to a wide audience while offering multiple levels of interpretation.

READY TO SOLVE THE MYSTERY?

"Last Detective Standing" offers a fresh and original perspective in the current television landscape, combining the comforting humor of a sitcom with the captivating intrigue of a mysterious dystopian world.
